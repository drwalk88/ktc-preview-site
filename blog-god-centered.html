Keeping God at the Center of Your Marriage

A marriage built on shared faith has a foundation that can weather any storm. Here's why keeping God at the center isn't just a nice idea — it's the key to everything.

Marriage Was God's Idea

Before anything else, it's important to remember that marriage didn't originate in culture, government, or tradition. Marriage was designed by God. Genesis 2:24 tells us, "For this reason a man will leave his father and mother and be united to his wife, and they will become one flesh." God created marriage as a sacred covenant — a reflection of His faithfulness, love, and commitment to His people.

At Keep The Covenant, we believe that marriage is a union between a man and a woman, created by God with the primary purpose of bringing Him glory. When we align our marriages with His design, we experience the fullness of what He intended for us.

Why a Christ-Centered Marriage Is Different

When both spouses are looking to Christ as the center of their relationship, something shifts. Instead of two people pulling in different directions, you have two people oriented toward the same source of truth, grace, and love. This doesn't mean your marriage will be free from conflict — but it means you have a shared foundation to return to when things get hard.

Ecclesiastes 4:12 puts it beautifully: "A cord of three strands is not quickly broken." You, your spouse, and God — that's the strongest foundation a marriage can have.

"The Christian does not think God will love us because we are good, but that God will make us good because He loves us." — C.S. Lewis

Practical Ways to Keep God at the Center

Pray together daily. It doesn't have to be long or formal. Simply coming before God together — even for two minutes — creates spiritual intimacy that nothing else can replicate. If you've never prayed together as a couple, start small. Hold hands and take turns thanking God for one thing about your spouse.

Read Scripture together. Pick a short book of the Bible and read a chapter each week. Discuss what stands out to you and how it applies to your relationship. Our Couples Prayer Guide (available on the Resources page) can help you get started.

Attend worship together. Being part of a church community reminds you that your marriage exists within a larger story — one that God is writing. Surround yourselves with other couples who share your values and can encourage you on the journey.

Serve together. Find a ministry, cause, or community need you can pour into as a couple. When you serve together, you experience a sense of shared purpose that deepens your bond and keeps your focus outward.

The Promise of a Covenant Marriage

Marriage isn't just a contract — it's a covenant. And God is a covenant keeper. When you commit your marriage to Him, you're not just making a promise to each other. You're entering into a sacred agreement witnessed by the Creator of marriage Himself. And He is faithful to sustain what He has joined together.
